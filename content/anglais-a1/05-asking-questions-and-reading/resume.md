# 📜 Résumé: Asking Questions & Short Reading

- **Question words** — _what_ (thing), _where_ (place), _when_ (time), _who_ (person), _why_ (reason), _how_ (manner), _whose_ (possession).
- **How many vs how much** — _how many_ + countable (_how many books_), _how much_ + uncountable (_how much water_).
- **Golden word order** — (question word) + auxiliary/be + subject + …: _Where is she? Who are they? What do you want?_
- **Questions with be** — just invert subject and verb, no helper: _Is she a nurse? Where is she?_
- **Questions with ordinary verbs** — add _do_ (I/you/we/they) or _does_ (he/she/it) + the **base** verb: _Where does he live?_
- ⚠️ Never **double-mark**: _Where does she live?_ (not _does she lives_); never drop the helper: not _Where she lives?_
- **Match question to answer** — a _Where_ question wants a place, a _When_ question a time, _Whose_ an owner.
- **Reading: find a detail** — the answer is written in the text; read it directly.
- **Reading: make an inference** — work out what the text implies, but only what the words **support**.
